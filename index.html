About the work.

My work sits at the intersection of simulation and physical validation — building models that are useful precisely because they have been checked against a prototype, a test rig, or a standard.

§ Project I · Industry R&D01

Vehicle Underbody Speed-Bump Envelope.

ClientMercedes-Benz R&D India RoleCAE Intern LocationBangalore PeriodMay — Jul 2023
Role
Independent CAE study under the Vehicle Validation group, owning the tool pipeline end-to-end.
Tooling
Simpack (multibody), MATLAB, OpenCRG (road profiles), IRC 99 standards.
Deliverables
Dimensional limit envelope · automated pipeline · reusable framework.

Defining a dimensional limit envelope for speed bumps that the entire passenger-car underbody can clear without contact — across three critical zones and a batch of IRC-standard profiles.

Problem01

Speed bumps in India vary wildly from the IRC 99 standard; a vehicle's underbody, bumpers and belly are vulnerable in ways the nominal bump geometry doesn't capture. The task: establish a single envelope that validation teams can use as a pass/fail check early in the design cycle, before hardware exists.

Approach02
  • Decomposed the underbody into three zones — front overhang (bumper to front axle), belly (between axles), rear overhang (rear axle to bumper) — each with independent geometric failure modes.
  • Built a Simpack multibody model of the car traversing parametrised IRC bumps; swept bump height, length and approach/departure angles in batch runs.
  • Generated the Grenzfläche (limit-surface) plot for each zone in MATLAB, then intersected the three surfaces into a unified envelope.
3 carlines
Validated across vehicle platforms, three load cases
10–20km/h
Traversal speed range swept in batch simulation
0 prototypes
Pipeline replaced physical prototype testing for this stage
Deliverable03
  • Delivered a fully validated dimensional limit envelope for speed bumps across 3 vehicle carlines, 3 load cases, and speeds of 10–20 km/h — broken down by front, middle, rear, and full-underbody contact zones.
  • The automated simulation pipeline replaced the need for physical prototype testing, cutting validation time and cost significantly.
  • Methodology was adopted as a reusable framework for future vehicle ground-clearance validation at MBRDI.
§ Project II · Industry R&D02

Halbach-Cylinder Magnetic Wheels — Design, Testing & Catalogue.

ClientL&T Heavy Engineering RoleR&D Intern LocationMumbai Period2023
Role
Design lead across the full lifecycle — magnetic-wheel module, shaft family, characterisation rig, and the final selection catalogue.
Tooling
Siemens NX, SolidWorks, FDM 3D printing, custom magnetic-force calculator, in-house test rig.
Output
Two-catalogue wheel-selection system · 5–110 kg payload range · reusable test rig.

Converting a theoretical Halbach-cylinder magnetisation pattern into a buildable, testable, and catalogued wheel family for adhesion-locomotive robots — from CAD through hardware to a reusable sizing chart.

Why Halbach01

A Halbach cylinder concentrates magnetic flux on one side of the array and nearly cancels it on the other — ideal for a wheel that has to grip a ferromagnetic surface without leaking field into the chassis or instrumentation above it. Two pole configurations were carried forward for comparison: quadro-polar and hexa-polar.

From CAD to Hardware02

Fabricated as prototypes using in-house FDM 3D printing with commercially available square-block magnets — replacing outsourced custom-magnet manufacturing and collapsing the unit cost. Dimensional inspection and pull-force checks were done in-house before moving to dynamic testing.

Test Rig & Shaft Family03

The existing test rig was built around a single wheel diameter and hub pattern. Designed a family of custom adapter shafts so one rig, sensor set and calibration carried across the whole wheel matrix — turning a one-off setup into a reusable test platform.

Catalogue — Wheel Sizing Chart04

With the rig stabilised across wheel variants, ran the full characterisation matrix and compiled a wheel-dimension-versus-payload-capacity catalogue — a sizing chart downstream designers can specify from.

Orthopedic Boot Thermal Biomechanics.

LabOrthopedic Biomechanics · BITS Pilani RoleResearch Assistant PeriodNov 2022 — Jan 2025 PublishedASME J. Thermal Science
Role
Research Assistant — instrumentation, participant testing, FEA validation, technical writing.
Tooling
Arduino, custom PCB with 12-thermistor array, Bluetooth logger, COMSOL Multiphysics, MATLAB.
Recognition
Director's Travel Grant ($1,500) · APOSM 2024 · IAAT 2024.

Measuring — and then simulating — the thermal field inside an orthopedic walking boot, so that design changes to ventilation and padding can be evaluated before clinical testing.

Experimental Setup01

Built a custom instrumentation stack: an Arduino-based logger reading twelve thermistors laid out anatomically (anterior and posterior, shin and calf), routed through a custom PCB and strapped beneath the boot. Readings were Bluetooth-logged and synchronised with activity state during the protocol.

Numerical Validation02

Built a COMSOL Multiphysics conjugate-heat-transfer model of the boot–leg assembly, using the instrumented data as a boundary condition and for validation of the simulated temperature field. The agreement between measured and simulated trends was the key milestone for using the model as a design tool.

§ Project V · Controls05

Model-Free Longitudinal Speed Controller.

ContextUT Dallas · Graduate RoleSole author Year2025
Role
Sole author — controller synthesis, implementation, and evaluation.
Tooling
MATLAB, Simulink — plant model, controller, Algebraic Differentiation Estimator (ADE) block.
Core Idea
The interesting half of the design is in the estimator, not the feedback law.

A controller that does not need a plant model it can trust — it only needs a clean estimate of the derivative. The interesting half of the design is in the estimator, not the feedback law.

The Idea01

Model-Free Control (MFC) replaces the unknown plant dynamics with an ultra-local model whose unknown part is estimated online from the input and output. That estimate is only useful if the derivative of the output can be recovered cleanly from a noisy signal — a problem where naïve finite differences catastrophically amplify noise.

Why ADE Matters02

Swapped naïve finite-difference differentiation for an Algebraic Differentiation Estimator built from integral operators that annihilate polynomial trends — giving a much smoother derivative estimate in the presence of measurement noise. The difference between the two approaches is the difference between a usable controller and an oscillating one.

§ Project VI · Ongoing Thesis06

Resilient Vector Consensus for Multi-Agent Systems.

LabCIRENs · UT Dallas TypeM.S. Thesis PeriodJun 2025 — present
Role
M.S. thesis research — algorithm analysis, MATLAB simulation, convergence & robustness evaluation.
Topics
Geometric median · Tukey depth · CPIH · Byzantine-resilient consensus · static adversary models.

Can a network of autonomous agents agree on a common value when some of them are faulty — or actively malicious — and no agent knows the full network topology?

Problem01

Distributed consensus is well understood when all agents are trustworthy. The interesting regime — and the one that matters for real systems — is when a bounded number of agents lie, fail or are compromised. Resilient vector consensus extends this from scalar values to points in Rᵈ, which is closer to what actual robot swarms need.

Current Directions02
  • Conducting research on resilient vector consensus algorithms focusing on geometric-median-based fault-tolerant consensus under a static adversary model.
  • MATLAB simulations to analyse convergence and robustness properties of the CPIH and Tukey-depth-based algorithms under parametric perturbations of the adversary strength.
  • Investigating how multi-agent systems can maintain consensus in the presence of Byzantine / faulty agents without global knowledge of network topology.
CPIH
Convex Polytope Intersection Hull — baseline algorithm
Tukey
Depth-based geometric filter for robust aggregation
Rd
Vector-valued agent state in d dimensions
Why It Matters03

The algorithms explored here have direct application to cooperative vehicle platoons, distributed robotic mapping, and any scenario where physical agents must reach agreement without a central coordinator they can trust. Each of those is a concrete mechanical-systems use case even though the maths lives in control theory.

§ Project VIII · Graduate FEA08

Pot-Type Bridge Bearing — Abaqus Contact FEA.

CourseMaterials Design & Manufacturing InstitutionUT Dallas PeriodJan — May 2025
Role
Sole author — geometry, material selection, contact-model definition, multi-step analysis and writeup.
Tooling
Abaqus / CAE — contact, nonlinear materials, multi-step loading (encastre base, vertical load, rotation).

Three load steps, one physically meaningful answer: the bearing has to carry vertical dead load, survive horizontal displacement and accommodate rotation without exceeding material allowables.

The Component01

A pot-type bridge bearing sits between the bridge deck and the pier. It transfers load vertically, but also has to tolerate deck rotation and horizontal translation driven by thermal expansion and live load. The confined elastomer pad inside the pot is what makes that combination possible.

Load Sequence02
  • Step 1 — fixes the base (encastre boundary condition).
  • Step 2 — applies a 1 MPa uniform vertical pressure on the sole.
  • Step 3 — adds a prescribed rotation to emulate deck thermal rotation.
  • Contact was defined between sole, PTFE pad, piston and pot wall.
